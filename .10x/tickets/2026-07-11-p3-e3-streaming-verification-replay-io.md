# P3 E3: bounded verification and replay I/O

## Scope

Stream manifest/segment verification, hash during consumer reads, bound/parallelize explicit verification, eliminate package-sized maps/vectors, and evaluate buffered/pread/mmap local replay with the unsafe gate closed by default.

## Acceptance criteria

- Run/replay destination reads verify exact segment bytes without a separate redundant pass.
- Explicit verify is bounded, parallel where beneficial, canonically reported, and detects all current tamper cases.
- Read-byte/syscall/copy/page-fault evidence selects the local strategy; mmap is absent unless separately ratified.
- High-cardinality and 1 TB replay remain within the memory budget.

## Evidence expectations

Tamper/golden parity, million-entry/RSS, buffered/pread/mmap evaluation, cold/warm profiles, cancellation/error cleanup, and destination-reader integration.

## Explicit exclusions

No unsafe/mmap without a new decision/fuzz target.

## Blockers

None. `.10x/decisions/capability-rooted-package-verification.md` ratifies the pinned cross-platform dependency and exact path/mutation authority; E2 and A5 are complete.

## Retrospective

- A seek-observing hash wrapper is not proof of exact Arrow IPC file coverage: the reader fetches the footer first and does not consume every identity byte. Fusion must be designed around sequential low-level decode and an authority that cannot reach final binding until every segment has acknowledged its exact digest.
- Shared-worktree dependency edits can invalidate `--locked` without implicating the scoped crate. An isolated offline source snapshot gave reproducible package evidence without mutating or waiting on another executor's lockfile ownership.
- Existing terminal evidence had already given the archive-force failures a durable owner. Searching before opening a follow-up prevented duplicate backlog.
- A fixed concurrency cap is not admission. Without governing resource authority, single-worker hashing is the bounded choice; parallelism belongs at a caller that can supply an admitted permit.
- Canonical reporting and bounded working memory can coexist without an in-memory sort by trading time on invalid inputs: repeated minimum selection is quadratic but retains one path. Valid packages require one discovery scan because no unexpected successor exists.
- The loaded manifest file vector can become the public report after hashes pass. Retaining per-file successes during verification was unnecessary duplication, especially on eventual failure.

## Review

### Findings

- **significant — bounded-memory claim is falsified by cardinality-proportional working results.** `verify_expected_file_chunk` reserves and retains one `checked_files` vector for every manifest chunk; `verify_expected_files_bounded` first collects all of those chunks and then allocates a second `checked_files` vector with capacity equal to the complete manifest before moving the entries into it. At merge time, successful verification therefore owns the public report allocation plus worker allocations whose total capacity is package-cardinality proportional. Invalid packages additionally retain per-worker failure vectors, a merged failure vector, and the final joined error string. Discovery also retains and sorts every unexpected path in `unexpected_paths`, so an adversarial package with many extras remains unbounded even if the manifest is small. This contradicts the journal statement that the v1 report is the only cardinality-proportional success allocation and does not meet the spec's bounded verification/no package-sized working vector requirement. The focused ordering test uses a small fixture and cannot expose this; the ticket correctly records that million-file verification RSS remains absent.
- **significant — unexpected symlinks and other non-files are not detected.** `visit_identity_file_paths` descends directories and visits only entries whose `file_type().is_file()` is true; symlinks, FIFOs, sockets, and other non-files are silently skipped. The worker correctly treats an expected path replaced by a symlink/directory as missing and never follows that symlink, but an extra symlink or non-file under an identity directory produces neither an `unexpected identity file` failure nor another verification failure. Regular missing, regular extra, and exact-byte tamper behavior remain preserved. This leaves the requested symlink/non-file/extra tamper surface incomplete and is not covered by the focused test.
- **significant — concurrency is capped but is not under memory/I/O admission.** `available_parallelism().min(4)` limits thread count, but the workers acquire no memory-ledger or I/O-admission lease before simultaneously opening and hashing files. A fixed thread cap is not the governing spec's required memory/I/O admission, and no high-cardinality or constrained-budget evidence establishes that four concurrent readers are beneficial or safe for the active budget.
- **minor — scoped-worker panic conversion is incomplete on short-circuit paths.** The join iterator stops at the first worker `Err` or converted panic. Remaining scoped handles are then dropped and automatically joined by `thread::scope`; if any unconsumed worker panics, `thread::scope` itself panics instead of returning `CdfError::internal("package verification worker panicked")`. All workers are still awaited, so cleanup is structurally scoped, but the advertised panic-to-error boundary is not guaranteed. No focused panic/error-cleanup assertion exercises this branch.
- **minor — canonical ordering is conditional on an unvalidated manifest invariant.** Valid builder output is path-sorted, and ordered handle joins preserve that order. However, the verifier explicitly accepts a self-consistent manifest whose `identity.files` vector is not sorted, falls back to linear membership checks, and emits checked-file and file-failure results in that noncanonical order. The focused test proves sorted extras precede two failures from an already canonical builder manifest; it does not prove or enforce canonical report/error order for a parseable noncanonical manifest.

### Verdict

**fail** for the bounded explicit-verification slice. Exact regular-file bytes are hashed with canonical ordering for normal builder-produced manifests, and regular missing/extra/tamper semantics are preserved, but the implementation still has package-cardinality-proportional working collections, lacks memory/I/O admission, and misses unexpected symlink/non-file entries. Those are direct governing-spec requirements rather than evidence-only residuals. Replay consumer-read fusion, million-file/RSS, 1 TB replay, and buffered/pread profiling remain openly unimplemented and were not treated as regressions in this review.

### 2026-07-12 Final focused re-review

#### Findings

- **critical — containment is still vulnerable to a component-check/open race.** `contained_expected_file` validates each pathname component with separate `symlink_metadata` calls, returns a `PathBuf`, and `file_entry_for_verified_path` subsequently re-resolves that pathname with ordinary `File::open`. A concurrently writable package can rename a checked intermediate directory and replace it with a symlink after its metadata check but before the leaf open. `File::open` then follows the replacement and hashes the outside target. A mismatch again includes the outside byte count and SHA-256; if the manifest already names the outside target's digest, verification can accept bytes reached outside the package. The same pathname-based design cannot bind the opened leaf to the directory objects that were inspected. The new intermediate-symlink test proves only a stable pre-existing symlink and cannot exercise this race. This is the prior critical trust-boundary failure narrowed to concurrent mutation, not eliminated. Closing it requires handle-relative component traversal/open with no-follow/beneath semantics appropriate to each supported platform, or an explicitly ratified immutable-package/exclusive-lock authority covering verification.
- **significant — lexical normalization does not establish canonical spelling on Windows-style filesystems.** `normalize_artifact_path(Path::new(path))` rejects parent/root/prefix components, backslash aliases on Windows, repeated separators, and `./` aliases. It does not reject filesystem aliases that remain `Component::Normal`, including NTFS alternate-data-stream spellings (`stats/file:stream`), case aliases on case-insensitive volumes, trailing-dot/space aliases, or reserved DOS names. A strictly sorted manifest can therefore name two strings that resolve to the same physical file or name an alternate stream; component checks and `File::open` use the platform interpretation. The macOS-focused tests cover parent, absolute, and dot aliases but provide no Windows/path-alias conformance. If those spellings are intentionally unsupported, the package path policy needs a platform-independent component grammar; if they are supported, canonical identity semantics require explicit ratification and cross-platform evidence.

No additional regression was found in the repaired stable-filesystem path:

- Parent traversal, absolute/root/prefix paths, empty/out-of-layout paths, and noncanonical dot/separator aliases fail before discovery or expected-file filesystem work. Because one invalid path sets `paths_are_safe = false`, the verifier does not hash any expected path and does not disclose an outside digest/byte count in these stable cases.
- A stable symlinked required or nested directory is detected component-by-component before leaf open, while leaf symlinks/non-files are treated as missing and unexpected non-files remain visible to discovery.
- Strict file-path ordering, lexical unexpected ordering, manifest-order missing/tamper diagnostics, exact regular-file hashing, the no-copy success-report move, single-threaded execution, and bounded verifier intermediates remain intact.

#### Verdict

**fail**. The repair closes stable parent/absolute/alias escapes and stable intermediate-symlink traversal, preserves bounded canonical verification, and prevents outside SHA/byte-count leakage in the focused stable cases. It does not provide race-safe containment between component validation and open, and its canonical spelling policy is incomplete on Windows-style filesystems. The critical package trust-boundary requirement therefore remains unmet.

Beyond the findings above, the verifier still has the general post-hash mutation window already inherent in issuing reusable verification authority for pathname-addressed files; even an atomically contained open would not by itself prove later consumers reopen the same inode/bytes. Million-entry RSS, 1 TB replay, consumer-read fusion, and local-I/O strategy evidence remain open ticket scope rather than claims of this repair.
